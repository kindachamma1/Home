My Bachelor Journey

A tale of learning across two universities.

Part 1: Avignon University

I began my Bachelor studies at Avignon University, where I was introduced to foundational concepts in computer science and mathematics.

I was enrolled in the CMI path, which stands for Cursus Master en Ingénierie Informatique. This program included 30% more coursework than a standard computer science degree, covering areas such as entrepreneurship, advanced mathematics, and research training.

Throughout the program, I took a variety of courses, ranging from programming and web development to computer systems and theoretical subjects such as foundations of computer science. Additionally, I participated in a specialized course composed of multiple research seminars, where I explored cutting-edge topics including NLP, IoT, and human–machine interaction.

Part 2: Linnaeus University

Later, I had the opportunity to study at Linnaeus University in Sweden.

This was a key experience for me, as I once again stepped outside my comfort zone to pursue studies abroad — and it was absolutely worth it.

During my time there, I completed four core courses:

  • Operating Systems
  • Object-Oriented Modeling with UML
  • Algorithms and Data Structures
  • Computer Security

Each course was equally valuable, offering both technical depth and practical experience. I also had the chance to meet and collaborate with many inspiring international peers, making it an enriching academic and cultural experience.
