My Bachelor Journey

A tale of learning across two universities.

Part 1: Avignon University

I began my Bachelor studies at Avignon University, where I was introduced to foundational concepts in computer science and mathematics. I was enrolled in the CMI path (Cursus Master en Ingénierie Informatique), which included 30% more coursework than a typical computer science degree. It also featured additional courses in entrepreneurship, advanced mathematics, and research training.

Below is an overview of the three academic years, using the French university semester notation where S1 is the first semester and S6 the last:

➡️ Year 1 – Semester 1 (S1)

This was my introduction to the field, heavily focused on mathematics and programming fundamentals.

  • UE Analyse 1: Functions, derivatives, integrals, and differential equations.
  • UE Algèbre 1: Logic, arithmetic, trigonometry, and complex numbers.
  • UE Bases de la Programmation: C++ basics, recursion, sorting algorithms, dynamic memory allocation, and pointers.
  • UE Fondements de l’Informatique: Boolean algebra, logical functions, simplification, and computer representation systems.
  • UE DBWEB 1 – Conception Internet: Introduction to the web, HTML, and CSS.
  • UE Anglais: English language coursework.
  • CMI 1 – Initiation à la Recherche (Projet Intégrateur): Research seminars on fields like NLP, IoT, and human–machine voice interaction.
